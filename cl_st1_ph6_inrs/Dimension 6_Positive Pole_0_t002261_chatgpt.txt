But as an individual with an educational background, and also a parent, I have seen a lot of instances where the parents are unresponsive to the teachers or flat out uninvolved in their child’s education. How do you intend to not only hold the teachers and schools accountable but also hold parents accountable?

Thank you for that important question. As a candidate who believes in the power of personal responsibility and local autonomy, I propose a different approach to education that respects the roles of both educators and parents without imposing excessive accountability measures.

Firstly, I believe that education should be seen as a personal investment. It is crucial that we empower parents to take charge of their children's educational journey. This means recognizing that parents, not the government, are best positioned to make decisions about their children's education, whether that involves choosing private schooling, homeschooling, or other educational paths that align with their values and resources.

In terms of accountability, I advocate for minimal standardization and limited government intervention. Schools and teachers should have the flexibility to innovate and tailor their teaching methods to the needs of their students without being constrained by rigid testing and accountability frameworks. This autonomy allows educators to focus on fostering individual student success rather than meeting standardized benchmarks.

Moreover, I support local control over education, which means that communities, not federal entities, should have the authority to manage their schools. This approach ensures that educational policies are responsive to the unique needs of each community, without unnecessary federal oversight.

Finally, I believe in market-driven solutions for teacher recruitment and retention. By reducing regulations and allowing for performance-based incentives, we can attract talented educators who are motivated by the opportunity to excel in a competitive environment.

In summary, my vision for education emphasizes personal and family responsibility, local autonomy, and minimal government involvement. By trusting parents and educators to make the best decisions for their children and students, we can create an educational system that truly prepares individuals for success in a competitive economy.
